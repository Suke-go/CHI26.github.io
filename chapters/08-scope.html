Reading note.

This page is about trust through claim boundaries. Instead of repeatedly listing what the method cannot do, we separate what is currently evidenced from what still requires additional material. That allows stronger discussion because confidence levels remain visible [1][2].

A key interpretive bridge from prior chapters: yearly summaries are independent computations from yearly concept spaces. They show annual structural condition, so they should be read as state descriptions rather than as identity-preserving trajectories of one persistent loop object.

What this evidence supports

These are directly tied to the observed outputs.

What requires additional evidence

These claims are discussable, but need more than topology outputs alone.

Technical note (optional): formal reading boundary for macro figures

This note clarifies why macro plots should not be interpreted as one-loop identity tracking.

For each year t, compute summaries from an independently built concept space V(t) and its derived topology.

What this means here: summaries at year t and year t+1 are comparable descriptors, but they do not certify that a single loop object is preserved with exact identity across years.
