Important caution.

Scope is best communicated as a continuum from strong support to explicit non-claims, not as a rhetorical split. The method directly supports reading semantic adjacency, persistent unresolved loops, and yearly structural summaries; it supports cautious interpretation of bridge hypotheses when combined with contextual knowledge; and it cannot establish causality, intent, institutional strategy, or unique historical truth for one pathway [1][2]. Presenting these boundaries in a single frame improves reliability because readers can trace where evidence is firm, where interpretation begins, and where claims should stop even when patterns appear compelling [3].
