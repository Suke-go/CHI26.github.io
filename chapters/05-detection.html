05. Detecting Boundary Structures

The detection workflow is intentionally simple and readable, moving from yearly distances to interpretable outputs at local, meso, and macro scales.

  1. Build a filtration from the distance matrix. Concepts are connected gradually by increasing a distance threshold, producing a growing simplicial structure.
  2. Extract persistent H_1 loops. Loop-like structures are identified and measured by how long they persist across thresholds.
  3. Reconstruct a representative cycle. Each loop is rendered in readable form using a birth edge and shortest-path reconstruction.
  4. Score candidate bridges. Concept pairs participating in representative cycles are scored as boundary-spanning bridge candidates.
  5. Build yearly summaries. Loop sets are aggregated into year-level descriptors such as total persistent H_1 and triadic closure.

Three reading scales: local edges, meso-scale representative cycles, and macro-scale yearly summaries.
