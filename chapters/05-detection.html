Evidence role. This schematic explains method sequence. It provides process evidence: how local outputs are transformed into meso descriptors and then annual summaries.

The pipeline is designed so every interpretive step has an upstream object you can inspect. A concept embedding relation can be checked locally, a persistent loop can be checked at meso structure level, and a yearly summary can be checked against those meso outputs [1][2].

A practical reading sequence is: (1) inspect one cycle in detail, (2) inspect bridge-linked edges in that cycle, and (3) inspect whether annual summaries show boundary-heavy or closure-heavy conditions. This keeps the macro narrative connected to concrete local artifacts.

Interpretive boundary: this is a descriptive structural method, not a forecasting dashboard. The gain is transparent boundary evidence that can be debated with domain knowledge rather than hidden in uninterpretable scores [3].

Scope note.
Technical note (optional): bridge score and yearly gap summary

These equations define how the method summarizes edge-level and year-level boundary strength.

s(u, v) = max over cycles gamma containing edge (u, v) of pi(gamma)
  • s(u, v): bridge score for edge (u, v).
  • pi(gamma): persistence of cycle gamma.

What this means here: an edge is treated as more boundary-relevant when it participates in highly persistent cycles.

Gaps(t) = sum of pi(gamma) over persistent H1 cycles above threshold tau
  • Gaps(t): yearly unresolved-boundary mass for year t.
  • tau: persistence threshold used to retain stable cycles.

What this means here: yearly summaries aggregate strong unresolved loops; they compare annual structural states and do not track one exact loop identity across time.
