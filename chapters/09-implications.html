What this means. The site is intended as a research discussion supplement: informative enough to evaluate claims, calm enough to remain readable.

The main implication is methodological: add a transparent structural lens to HCI and Science of Science workflows without replacing qualitative interpretation. Representative cycles make candidate boundaries inspectable, ranking evidence adds comparative support, and yearly summaries offer field-condition context [1][2][3].

A second implication is epistemic: claim boundaries should be part of the method, not an afterthought. By keeping direct evidence, interpretation, and open questions separate, the project makes disagreement more productive and easier to audit.

Three discussion anchors

What is being detected

The core object is persistent H1 boundary structure in yearly concept space. This is evidence of unresolved reorganization patterns, not proof of durable field formation by itself.

What counts as evidence

Evidence is strongest when cycle-level interpretation, metric behavior, and yearly summaries converge while staying consistent with declared scope boundaries.

What remains open for discussion

Interpretation still requires disciplinary judgment when moving from structural descriptors to historical, institutional, or normative claims.

Discussion Agenda

This chapter proposes a focused agenda for HCI and Science of Science discussion rather than a final verdict.

  1. What should count as meaningful reorganization in HCI? Topic growth alone may miss early restructuring signals. Persistent unresolved boundary structures may also indicate meaningful change before naming convergence.
  2. How much interpretability should structural methods provide? Representative cycles are readable witnesses, but not unique truths. A key discussion point is what level of explanation is sufficient for descriptive structural claims.
  3. How should local, meso, and macro evidence be combined? Case-level interpretation, ranking evidence, and yearly summaries should mutually constrain each other so no layer is over-interpreted in isolation.
  4. When does a boundary become a field? Some boundary structures consolidate into durable integration, while others fade or fragment. The community needs clearer criteria for durable emergence.

This site is not meant to settle these questions. It is meant to provide a clearer descriptive basis for discussing them.
