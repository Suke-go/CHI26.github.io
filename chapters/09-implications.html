9-1. Implications for HCI

For researchers: High-persistence voids mark regions where integration is not merely absent but actively resisted. Bridging such voids requires explicit methodological negotiation, not just technical novelty. The representative cycle provides a map of which intermediate concepts might facilitate that negotiation.

For reviewers and program committees: A submission on a persistent void boundary should not be evaluated as “between communities” (often a rejection rationale) but as a potential bridge requiring expertise from both sides. The persistence score provides a structural measure of how significant the void is.

For the field’s self-reflection: The pattern of voids—which persist, which close, which emerge—constitutes a structural signature of HCI’s evolution. The shift from 2005’s cognitive-methodological loops to 2023’s cross-paradigmatic tensions (AI ↔ ethics ↔ social computing) suggests HCI is navigating increasingly complex structural terrain.

9-2. Implications for Science of Science

This work contributes interpretable structural descriptors to the SciSci toolkit. Unlike topic models or co-citation networks, persistent homology measures what resists connection, not what is connected. The representative cycle mechanism turns abstract topological features into readable, auditable descriptions of field structure.

The approach generalizes beyond HCI: any field with concept-level embeddings and temporal corpus data could apply the same pipeline.

9-3. Future directions

  • Void dynamics: Track how specific voids emerge, persist, and close over time to reveal the “structural metabolism” of a field.
  • Review outcome correlation: Do papers on high-persistence void boundaries receive more split reviews?
  • Cross-field comparison: Compare HCI’s void structure with psychology, design, or CS theory.
  • Expert validation: Structured evaluation of bridge cards by domain experts.
  • Aligned embeddings: Time-aware or aligned embeddings to enable cross-year tracking of specific features.
Position. This work is a diagnostic mirror for the community—not an oracle that predicts trends, but a tool that reveals where the field’s structural tensions lie, enabling more reflective decision-making about which integrations to pursue and which separations to maintain.
