09. Implications for HCI and SciSci

This chapter translates the method into practical implications for readers who need bridge hypotheses, community-level reflection, and Science of Science framing.

For HCI researchers

Representative cycles can be used as bridge hypotheses: readable suggestions about how areas may be becoming adjacent through intermediate concepts before topic labels stabilize.

For HCI community analysis

Yearly topology-derived summaries can support reflection on whether the field is accumulating unresolved boundaries or closing them through stronger structural integration.

For Science of Science

The approach complements frequency-based indicators by describing structural reorganization, not only visible trend volume.

Future work

Next steps include expert validation of representative cases, extension to broader corpora, and improved temporal alignment methods.
