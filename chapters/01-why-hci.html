A. Hero / top section

Research question. Can yearly topological structure in HCI concept space reveal boundary reorganization before a topic has a stable label or frequent direct co-occurrence?

Why this is non-trivial. Most annual topic summaries are strongest after terms have already consolidated. This project targets earlier boundary states where relation patterns are emerging but still structurally unresolved.

Evidence in this companion. We combine (i) yearly geometry, (ii) persistent-loop structure, (iii) representative-cycle edge extraction, and (iv) future-year relevance checks. The strongest interpretation is where all four align.

B. Why this question matters in HCI

HCI evolves through frequent recombination across methods, domains, and contexts. If analysis relies only on yearly topic labels, early cross-boundary reorganization can be under-described because labels stabilize after substantial consolidation.

Direct co-occurrence in later years is informative but incomplete as an early signal: two ideas may become structurally adjacent through intermediate concepts before direct pairing becomes frequent. For HCI, this matters because many influential connections emerge through design, methods, and application layers before a canonical term dominates.
