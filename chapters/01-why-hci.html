Scope note.

In HCI, important change often appears first as partial adjacency, not as a stable topic label. Design, systems, social inquiry, and domain work move at different speeds, so boundary change can become visible before vocabulary converges [1][2].

This project uses persistent loops to describe that intermediate state. A loop here means multiple concept neighborhoods are linked around a still-open gap. That gives a concrete way to discuss reorganization without jumping straight to strong field-formation claims [3][4].

Stable topics are still important, but they are often retrospective. Early boundary activity can be structurally meaningful even when one dominant label has not yet formed.
