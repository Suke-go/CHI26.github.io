01. Why This Matters in HCI

Influential work in HCI often emerges at boundaries: between methods and domains, between devices and practices, and between technical systems and real-world settings. Because boundary states often precede stable named topics, understanding them is central to interpreting how HCI reorganizes over time.

Why HCI is a boundary-rich field

HCI continuously combines design, engineering, behavioral research, health, education, and many application domains. This pluralism is productive, but it also means structural change can appear first as subtle shifts in adjacency across concept regions rather than as immediate growth in a single established topic label.

Why co-occurrence is often too late

Co-occurrence and frequency-based maps are useful once a direction has consolidated. However, they tend to become informative after vocabulary repetition and citation accumulation are already visible. That makes them strong for established trend reading, but weaker for early boundary formation.

What this project asks instead

This project does not ask whether topology is interesting in itself. It asks whether topology helps describe pre-topic reorganization in interpretable terms: not only which concepts are close, but where unresolved structural gaps remain between areas that may be moving toward integration.

Takeaway: the target is not “future topic prediction” as a single label outcome, but readable characterization of boundary formation before stable topic visibility.
