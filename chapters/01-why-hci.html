Why HCI is boundary-rich

HCI draws from design, engineering, social science, health, education, and domain practice. Because of that pluralism, early reorganization often appears as changing neighborhood structure across communities rather than immediate dominance of one shared label. A boundary view is therefore useful for reading early-stage shifts that topic-frequency maps may miss.

Why frequency maps are often too late

Co-occurrence and citation volume become strong once repetition and visibility are already established. They are excellent for consolidated topics, but less sensitive to unresolved structure where adjacent areas have not yet closed into dense direct ties.

What this project asks instead

The core question is whether persistent H1 cycles can make boundary formation inspectable: where concepts become adjacent through intermediates, while integration remains incomplete.
