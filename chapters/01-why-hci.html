Research question and intuition

Plain-language layer: Can we detect boundary reorganization in HCI before a field name stabilizes?

Technical layer: Given yearly concept spaces, we test whether persistent H1 loop structure and cross-community cycle edges provide early, interpretable bridge candidates that receive future relevance support.

Yearly topic labels are often retrospective; they detect consolidation after vocabulary converges. This supplement instead targets transient boundary states: neighborhoods that are increasingly connected but still structurally unresolved.

What counts as evidence here? A claim is strongest when four elements align: local neighborhood geometry, persistent-loop structure, cross-community cycle edge extraction, and future-year relevance in ranked evaluation.
