Ranking function and holdout logic

s(u,v)=max_{\gamma\ni (u,v)} π(γ), with π(γ)=ε_d(γ)−ε_b(γ)

Interpretation: an edge is ranked higher if it participates in more persistent cycles.

Temporal validation: candidates generated at year t are judged using future evidence from years t+1 onward, so ranking quality is evaluated out-of-time.

How to read nDCG@50

Plain-language layer: nDCG@50 asks whether candidates that later mattered were already near the top 50.

Technical layer: DCG discounts relevance by rank position; nDCG normalizes by the ideal ordering so 1.0 means best possible top-50 ordering for that year.
