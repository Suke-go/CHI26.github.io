06. Evidence

This project uses three kinds of evidence. At the micro level, representative cases show how a boundary can be interpreted through intermediate concepts. At the meso level, ranking metrics test whether these bridges behave competitively as early signals. At the macro level, yearly summaries describe whether concept space contains many unresolved loop structures or greater closure.

Micro: representative case

Example bridge hypothesis: VR to Therapy. A representative cycle can include intermediate concepts such as exposure design, embodied interaction, and clinical protocol terms. This suggests a structured adjacency pathway before a single stable topic identity dominates. It does not prove causal diffusion, intentional collaboration, or a unique route of integration.

Meso: evaluation

P@50 evaluates precision among top-ranked bridge candidates, and NDCG evaluates ranking quality with positional weighting. Competitive ranking matters because a method should surface plausible early bridge signals near the top, not only somewhere in a long list. Interpretability also matters: representative cycles provide explanatory structure beyond raw scores.

Macro: yearly descriptors

Total persistence summarizes how much unresolved loop structure exists in a year. Triadic closure summarizes the degree to which local neighborhoods are filling in. Read together, they characterize whether the yearly concept space looks boundary-heavy or closure-heavy.

Important: yearly indicators are yearly topological summaries, not identity tracking of the same loop across time.
