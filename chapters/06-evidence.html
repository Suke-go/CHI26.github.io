6-1. Micro: representative cases

VR – Therapy (Integration)

In 2013, “VR” (hardware/design community) and “Therapy” (clinical/wellbeing community) were in separate Louvain communities with zero co-occurrence. TDA detected a boundary loop with the birth edge (vr, therapy).

  • Representative cycle path: vr → immersion → exergames → therapies → therapy
  • Lead time: 3 years (detected 2013, co-occurrence ≥5 by 2016)
  • Outcome: By 2019, “VR Healthcare” became an established CHI track.

Blockchain – Participatory Design (Decay)

In 2018, a bridge formed between “Blockchain” and “Participatory Design.” The structural hole did not fill; the bridge dissolved by 2021 as topics drifted apart. The method correctly classified this as decay.

Interpretation. Of dissolved bridges, 89.5% followed integration (Scenario A). Decay cases like Blockchain–PD are the minority but illustrate the method’s discriminative capability.

Graph-structure methods (Triangle Deficit, Link Prediction) achieve higher integration rates because they target edges where connectivity evidence already exists. H₁ bridges target structural voids before edges form—a fundamentally different and complementary signal (overlap with Louvain Cross-Edges: only 6.4%).

Lead time

Among 267 detected bridges (2010–2020): median lead time = 2 years (IQR: 1–3). 40.9% integrated within 1 year; 86.5% within 3 years.

6-3. Macro: yearly structure

Yearly H₁ summaries reveal a structural pattern across HCI eras:

  • 2005: Dominant loop concepts were patterns, multimodal integration, educational—second-wave cognitive/systematic design.
  • 2015: Shifted to mobile, haptic, touch, gaze—embodied, tangible, ubiquitous interaction.
  • 2023: Current loops connect mental health, social connectedness, misinformation, generative AI, privacy/security—societal impact and AI ethics.

The structural shape of voids itself changes: 2005 loops were localized within methodological clusters, while 2023 loops span between paradigmatic communities (AI/ML vs. social computing vs. critical HCI).

Note. These are yearly topology-derived summary measures, not direct tracking of the same loop across time. Each year’s topology is computed independently.
