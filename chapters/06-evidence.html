Important caution.

Evidence is strongest when read across three linked scales. At micro scale, a representative cycle provides one concrete case of boundary adjacency becoming inspectable; at meso scale, ranking metrics test whether those structural signals improve candidate bridge ordering; at macro scale, yearly summaries indicate whether unresolved boundary conditions are broadly intensifying or relaxing in that year [1][2]. This continuity avoids overstatement: the case shows interpretability, the metric block shows comparative performance, and the macro note supports contextual interpretation rather than causal attribution or deterministic forecasting, institutional explanation, or author-level motivation claims [3].
