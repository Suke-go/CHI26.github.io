H. Ranking and temporal validation

What is ranked. The ranked objects are candidate edges (u,v), not full topics, people, or venues.

s(u,v) = maxγ ∋ (u,v) π(γ), π(γ) = εd(γ) − εb(γ)

This score gives each candidate edge the strongest persistence evidence among cycles that contain it; higher score prioritizes edges participating in longer-lived loop structures.

DCG@50 = Σr=1..50(2relr−1)/log₂(r+1), nDCG@50 = DCG@50 / IDCG@50

DCG discounts relevance by rank position, and nDCG normalizes by the best possible ordering for that year so values are comparable.

Temporal protocol. Generate candidate rankings at year t; compute relevance only from future years t+1, t+2, .... This keeps evaluation out-of-time relative to candidate generation.

nDCG@50 (quick definition). nDCG@50 asks whether candidates that later turned out to matter were already placed near the top of the earlier ranked list. Items near rank 1 contribute more than items near rank 50 because of logarithmic discounting. A value closer to 1 means the ordering is closer to the ideal top-50 ordering.
