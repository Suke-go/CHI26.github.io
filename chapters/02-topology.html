Why persistence alone is not enough

Plain-language layer: A long-lived loop tells us a boundary stayed open, but not which relation is the most plausible bridge.

Technical layer: Persistence identifies durable H1 structure, but bridge recommendation additionally needs cycle reconstruction and community-aware edge selection.

Why community partition is introduced

Plain-language layer: We need to separate within-area edges from across-area edges.

Technical layer: A community partition on the separation graph lets us identify cycle edges (u,v) where endpoints have different community labels, defining candidate bridges.

Why a loop is not automatically a future topic. A loop is an observed structural condition. It does not by itself establish durable future collaboration, topic naming, or causal mechanism.

Interpretation checklist

  • Did the loop appear at a specific filtration threshold?
  • Was a representative cycle reconstructed from a documented birth edge?
  • Are extracted candidate edges cross-community in that year?
  • Is future relevance evaluated in a temporally held-out window?
  • Does interpretation stay within descriptive and retrospective claim limits?
