What this means. Birth, persistence, and closure are used here as a minimal interpretive grammar for reading changing boundary structure over time.

In this project, topology is a way to summarize connectedness and open boundary structure in yearly concept spaces rather than a geometric claim about exact shapes. A birth edge marks the threshold where a loop becomes visible, persistence tracks how long that loop remains unresolved, and death indicates local closure in the filtration [1][2]. These terms support continuity from local loop reading to macro yearly summary because each year is interpreted as a structural condition, not a timeless object identity. Representative cycles are therefore explanatory reconstructions that help communicate evidence while preserving uncertainty about unique historical pathways [3].
