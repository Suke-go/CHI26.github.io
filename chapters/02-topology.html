02. Topology: Minimal Background

Topology here means structure, not geometry. The method tracks patterns of connectedness in concept space: what becomes linked, what remains separated, and where loop-like boundaries persist before closure.

Local scale: a new edge appears

A birth edge is the connection whose appearance helps create a loop. At this local scale, the signal is not “these two concepts are always related.” Instead, it is that local proximity has become structurally important in the broader neighborhood.

Meso scale: a loop exists before the middle is filled

A persistent loop means concepts can form a ring-like relation while key shortcuts are still missing. We interpret this as an unresolved boundary: adjacency is emerging through intermediate concepts, but complete closure has not yet occurred.

Macro scale: a year can be summarized

Year-level descriptors such as total persistent H_1 and triadic closure summarize each year’s structural state. These values do not assume one loop identity persists through time; they compare independently computed yearly topological summaries.

Interpretive caution

A representative cycle is a readable reconstruction, not a unique true pathway. It supports explanation and hypothesis generation, but does not prove that integration happened through one exact historical route.

Technical note (optional)

We use a Vietoris–Rips filtration over yearly concept distances and read H_1 birth/death behavior as evidence of changing boundary structure.
