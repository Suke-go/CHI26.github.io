What this means. These three terms are a reading protocol, helping interpretation move from local evidence to yearly commentary with fewer leaps.

This chapter provides a practical reading grammar for outputs seen earlier. Birth indicates when a boundary condition becomes topologically visible, persistence indicates the condition remains unresolved across thresholds, and closure indicates local filling that can accompany integration or dispersion depending on surrounding evidence [1][2]. The key is continuity: read representative cycles as explanatory slices of structure, then connect them to yearly summaries without claiming one-to-one historical identity. This supports measured interpretation of field dynamics in practice, during review and discussion, while keeping causal, institutional, and motivational claims outside the method’s evidential reach [3].

Important caution. Not every disappearing loop becomes a stable field; some dissolve without durable consolidation.
