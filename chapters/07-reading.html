What this means. Use these terms as simple reading states, not as a full historical model.

Birth means the boundary becomes visible. Persistence means the boundary stays unresolved. Closure means local filling appears. Some closures support stable integration; others fade or diverge [1][2].

Read one representative cycle first, then check yearly summaries. This keeps the interpretation grounded and avoids claiming that one exact loop identity was tracked across years. The goal is clear reading confidence, not over-interpretation [3].

Scope note. Not every disappearing loop becomes a stable field.
