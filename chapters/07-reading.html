07. How to Read the Outputs

This chapter provides an interpretive grammar so outputs are read as structural evidence with explicit limits.

Birth

Birth marks the emergence of a boundary condition: a structurally meaningful loop becomes visible in the filtration.

Persistence

Persistence suggests the boundary remains unresolved. Areas are adjacent through intermediate structure but not yet fully integrated.

Death

Death indicates the loop has been filled in, often corresponding to increased closure and sometimes preceding stronger direct co-occurrence.

Caution: death is not guaranteed success. Some boundaries consolidate into durable links; others disappear without producing a stable new area.

Representative cycles

Representative cycles are explanatory units. They do not prove a unique historical path, but they make structural readings inspectable and discussable.
