Important caution. Cross-area here does NOT mean institutional interdisciplinarity.

Operational clarity is essential for interpretation. A concept is a noun-phrase-like textual unit extracted from titles and abstracts; a semantic community is a data-driven cluster in yearly embedding space; a cross-area link is adjacency connecting concepts from different communities through structural neighborhoods; a representative cycle is an explanatory reconstruction of one persistent loop; and a yearly topology-derived summary is an annual aggregate descriptor of loop persistence and closure tendency [1][2]. These definitions anchor claims to corpus structure only, preventing drift into causal, institutional, or motivational interpretation that the data cannot directly establish [3][4].
