Scope note. Here, cross-area refers to relations across semantic communities in this corpus, not formal institutional interdisciplinarity.

Operational definitions are the backbone of interpretability in this project. Each major term is restricted to a measurable object so that readers can see exactly what is observed, what is summarized, and where interpretation should stop [1][2].

Operational definitions used in this project

Concept

Definition in this project: a noun-phrase-like textual unit extracted from paper titles and abstracts in a given year.

Observable/measured object: the extracted tokenized phrase and its position in yearly embedding space after preprocessing.

Caution: this is not a direct measure of full theoretical constructs, author intentions, or downstream practical impact.

Semantic community

Definition in this project: a data-driven cluster of concepts in yearly semantic embedding space.

Observable/measured object: community assignments generated from graph structure over concept neighborhoods for that year.

Caution: community labels should not be over-read as fixed disciplinary categories with stable sociological boundaries.

Cross-area link

Definition in this project: an adjacency relation connecting concepts that belong to different semantic communities.

Observable/measured object: an edge in the yearly concept graph whose endpoints have different community memberships.

Caution: this does not establish inter-institutional collaboration, funding coordination, or deliberate strategic integration.

Representative cycle

Definition in this project: an explanatory reconstruction used to communicate one persistent H1 boundary pattern.

Observable/measured object: a cycle selected from persistent homology outputs and mapped to interpretable concept terms.

Caution: this is a readable witness, not proof that one unique historical pathway or singular causal mechanism exists.

Yearly topology summary

Definition in this project: annual aggregate descriptors of unresolved loop persistence and closure tendency.

Observable/measured object: year-indexed statistics such as persistent H1 totals and triadic closure indicators.

Caution: yearly summaries characterize structural condition for that year and should not be interpreted as identity tracking of the same loop across years [3][4].

Why these definitions matter for later figures: each figure in Chapters 5–8 asks you to read a specific observable object (cycle, ranking card, yearly summary). These definitions keep that reading consistent, so interpretation stays connected to measurable outputs.
