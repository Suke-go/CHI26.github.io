4-1. Corpus

  • Venues: CHI, UIST, CSCW, UbiComp
  • Time span: 1999–2024 (~30,000 papers)
  • Temporal honesty: Each year t uses only data available up to t. No future leakage.

4-2. Concept extraction

Concepts are noun phrases extracted from titles and abstracts using spaCy. Stopwords and rare concepts (appearing in fewer than 5 papers cumulatively) are filtered. Top 500 concepts by frequency are retained for each year.

4-3. Year-specific embeddings

For each year t, a Word2Vec model (d=100, window=5, min_count=5) is trained on the cumulative corpus up to t. Distance is angular distance: dij = arccos(cos_sim) / π.

4-4. Semantic communities

A k-NN graph (k=15) is built from the distance matrix. Community detection uses the Louvain algorithm on an ε-separated graph (where ε is chosen at the 30% largest-component threshold). This partitions concepts into semantic communities.

4-5. Candidate pairs

A candidate pair must satisfy two conditions:

  1. Cross-community: The two concepts belong to different Louvain communities.
  2. Zero cumulative co-occurrence: The pair has never co-occurred in the same paper up to year t.
Important. “Cross-area link” here means a boundary between semantic communities in concept space. It does not mean institutional interdisciplinarity or collaboration between departments.
