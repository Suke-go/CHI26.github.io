3-1. Citation-based mappings

Citation analysis, co-citation, and bibliographic coupling reveal intellectual lineage and knowledge clusters. CiteSpace (Chen, 2006) visualizes citation bursts and emerging fronts. These methods are strongest at capturing established knowledge structures and influential works.

3-2. Co-word and co-occurrence mappings

Co-word networks built from titles, abstracts, or keywords identify visible thematic clusters and their evolution. VOSviewer (van Eck & Waltman, 2010) is widely used for this. Co-occurrence methods excel at mapping what is connected, but they inherently rely on terms that already appear together in publications.

3-3. Topic and semantic mappings

Topic models (LDA; Blei et al., 2003) and semantic embeddings (Word2Vec; Mikolov et al., 2013) capture latent themes and semantic proximity. These methods can suggest what could be connected based on vector similarity, even without direct co-occurrence.

3-4. Graph-based prediction

Link prediction methods (Adamic-Adar, Resource Allocation, common neighbors) and triangle-closing heuristics predict missing edges based on existing graph density. These are powerful for near-term closure prediction where shared neighbors already exist.
