CHI 2026 Supplement


This visual essay explains how topological summaries can reveal boundary reorganization inside HCI before emerging areas become obvious as stable named topics. The corpus covers approximately 30,000 papers across CHI, UIST, CSCW, and UbiComp from 1999–2025 (CSCW through 2024), and focuses on persistent H1 cycles as signals of unresolved structural gaps.

Site Promise

  • What this site shows: how yearly concept-space topology can surface structural boundaries and bridge candidates.
  • What this site does not claim: causality, author intent, institutional interdisciplinarity, or one unique true path.
  • What you will learn: how to read local loops, meso-level ranking evidence, and macro yearly summaries together.

Three discussion anchors

What is being detected

The primary object is a persistent H1 loop in yearly concept space. In this project, that loop is interpreted as an unresolved boundary condition: multiple concept neighborhoods are linked around a gap, but closure edges are still missing.

What counts as evidence

Evidence is cumulative across scales: (1) an inspectable representative cycle at micro scale, (2) comparative ranking improvements at meso scale, and (3) yearly topology-derived summaries at macro scale. A claim is stronger when these scales converge rather than when one indicator appears in isolation.

What remains open for discussion

Interpretive judgment remains necessary when moving from structural signal to field-level meaning. The method can identify plausible boundary reorganization, but discussion is still needed to decide whether a specific pattern reflects meaningful integration, temporary alignment, or only local vocabulary drift.
