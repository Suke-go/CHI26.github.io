CHI 2026 Poster Companion Website


Plain-language summary. This companion site explains how yearly topology of HCI concept space is used to identify bridge candidates: concept pairs that sit on unresolved boundaries between semantic communities before direct links become common. The goal is not to predict the future from one signal, but to provide structurally interpretable candidates and evaluate whether later evidence tends to support highly ranked ones.

Technical summary. For each year t, we construct a historical concept space from papers published up to year t, compute a Vietoris–Rips filtration, extract persistent H_1 intervals, reconstruct representative cycles from birth events, select cross-community cycle edges as candidates, rank those edges by persistence-based scores, and validate rankings against future-year relevance using nDCG@50.

At a glance

  • Data scope: approximately 30,000 papers from CHI, UIST, CSCW, and UbiComp.
  • Year range: 1999–2025 (CSCW coverage through 2024 in the current dataset snapshot).
  • Temporal cutoff logic: each year t is recomputed using only information available up to t.
  • Main analytical output: ranked cross-community bridge candidates derived from representative cycle edges.
  • Evaluation horizon: future-year relevance and ordering quality measured with nDCG@50 and lead-time summaries.

Evidence type: the site provides observational structural evidence plus retrospective temporal validation; it does not claim causal discovery or guaranteed future topic emergence.
