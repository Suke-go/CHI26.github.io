Topological Bridges
A visual supplement for understanding boundary formation inside HCI

This site asks a simple question: how can we detect meaningful reorganization inside HCI before it becomes visible as a stable named topic? Rather than relying only on co-occurrence volume, this project examines the shape of concept space. Persistent loops are treated as signals of unresolved boundary structure, where areas become adjacent through intermediate concepts but are not yet fully integrated.

Site Promise

  • What this site shows: Topology-derived boundary structures in the HCI concept space.
  • What it does not claim: Causal proof, author intent, or a single true path of integration.
  • What you will learn: How local loop structures, yearly summaries, and representative cases fit together.

Hero Figure (Schematic Animation)

The animation is explanatory rather than analytic: it shows a conceptual sequence from isolated regions to loop formation and closure, not a direct empirical result.

How to Read This Site

Read from top to bottom. Chapters 1–3 define the problem and position the method. Chapters 4–6 explain what is measured and what the evidence looks like. Chapters 7–9 clarify interpretation, limits, and implications for HCI and Science of Science.
