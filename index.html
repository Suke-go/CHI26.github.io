CHI 2026 Poster Companion Website


Poster recap. This site asks whether yearly structure in HCI concept space can show boundary reorganization before direct co-occurrence becomes common and stable topic labels appear.

Additional notes. This site keeps terminology fixed. The two main terms are boundary loop and bridge candidate. It then adds concrete definitions, equations, and evaluation steps.

Animated intuition

This animation gives a quick orientation. Local proximity appears first. Boundary loop structure can persist. Closure may happen later.

At a glance

  • Data scope: approximately 30,000 papers from CHI, UIST, CSCW, and UbiComp.
  • Year range: 1999–2025 (CSCW coverage through 2024 in the current dataset snapshot).
  • Temporal cutoff logic: each year t is recomputed using only information available up to t.
  • Main analytical output: ranked cross-community bridge candidates derived from representative cycle edges.
  • Evaluation horizon: future-year relevance and ordering quality measured with nDCG@50 and lead-time summaries (how many years earlier a useful signal appears).

Evidence type: the site provides observational structural evidence plus retrospective temporal validation; it does not claim causal discovery or guaranteed future topic emergence.

Baseline definition (Science of science)

Working premise for this companion. Science of science is an interdisciplinary field that studies how science works by using large-scale data and computational methods. It covers publication/citation patterns, collaboration, peer review, social bias, and policy-facing use of research evidence.

This companion is positioned inside that tradition. Our specific contribution is a structural method for detecting cross-community bridge candidates from yearly concept-space topology and evaluating them with future-year relevance.

Read in order

Chapters 1–9 are organized to answer a reviewer's core questions from motivation to mathematical definition, ranking logic, case interpretation, claim boundaries, and reference integrity.

Reading options. Each chapter starts with plain-language scope and then moves to formulas, figures, and evaluation steps.

Designed for reviewer legibility: explicit objects, explicit equations, explicit boundaries of interpretation.
